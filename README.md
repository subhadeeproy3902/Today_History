## Year - 2023

## Year - 2016
 Eighty-eight thousand people are evacuated from their homes in [Fort McMurray](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Fort_McMurray "Fort McMurray"), Alberta, Canada as [a wildfire rips through the community](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_Fort_McMurray_Wildfire "2016 Fort McMurray Wildfire"), destroying approximately 2,400 homes and buildings.

## Year - 2015
 Two gunmen [launch an attempted attack](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Curtis_Culwell_Center_attack "Curtis Culwell Center attack") on an anti-Islam event in [Garland, Texas](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Garland,_Texas "Garland, Texas"), which was held in response to the [<i>Charlie Hebdo</i> shooting](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Charlie_Hebdo_shooting "Charlie Hebdo shooting").

## Year - 2007
 The three-year-old British girl [Madeleine McCann](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Disappearance_of_Madeleine_McCann "Disappearance of Madeleine McCann") disappears in Praia da Luz, Portugal, starting "the most heavily reported missing-person case in modern history".

## Year - 2006
 [Armavia Flight 967](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Armavia_Flight_967 "Armavia Flight 967") crashes into the [Black Sea](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea "Black Sea") near [Sochi International Airport](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Sochi_International_Airport "Sochi International Airport") in [Sochi](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Sochi "Sochi"), Russia, killing 113 people.

## Year - 2001
 The United States loses its seat on the [U.N. Human Rights Commission](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/U.N._Human_Rights_Commission "U.N. Human Rights Commission") for the first time since the commission was formed in [1947](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/1947 "1947").

## Year - 2000
 The sport of [geocaching](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Geocaching "Geocaching") begins, with the first cache placed and the coordinates from a [GPS](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Positioning_System "Global Positioning System") posted on [Usenet](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Usenet "Usenet").

## Year - 1999
 Infiltration of Pakistani soldiers on Indian side results in the [Kargil War](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Kargil_War "Kargil War").

## Year - 1987

## Year - 1986

## Year - 1979

The following day, she becomes the first female [British Prime Minister](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom "Prime Minister of the United Kingdom").

## Year - 1978
 The first unsolicited bulk commercial [email](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Email "Email") (which would later become known as "[spam](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Spam_(electronic) "Spam (electronic)")") is sent by a [Digital Equipment Corporation](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_Equipment_Corporation "Digital Equipment Corporation") marketing representative to every [ARPANET](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/ARPANET "ARPANET") address on the west coast of the United States.

## Year - 1971
 [Erich Honecker](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Erich_Honecker "Erich Honecker") becomes [First Secretary of the Socialist Unity Party of Germany](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Leadership_of_East_Germany#Leaders_of_the_Socialist_Unity_Party_of_Germany_(SED) "Leadership of East Germany"), remaining in power until 1989.

## Year - 1968
 Eighty-five people are killed when [Braniff International Airways Flight 352](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Braniff_International_Airways_Flight_352 "Braniff International Airways Flight 352") crashes near [Dawson, Texas](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Dawson,_Texas "Dawson, Texas").

## Year - 1963
 The police force in [Birmingham, Alabama](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Birmingham,_Alabama "Birmingham, Alabama") switches tactics and [responds with violent force](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Birmingham_campaign#Fire_hoses_and_police_dogs "Birmingham campaign") to stop the "[Birmingham campaign](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Birmingham_campaign "Birmingham campaign")" protesters. Images of the violent suppression are transmitted worldwide, bringing new-found attention to the [civil rights movement](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Civil_rights_movement "Civil rights movement").

## Year - 1957

## Year - 1953

Amateur photographer [Virginia Schau](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Virginia_Schau "Virginia Schau") photographs "Rescue on Pit River Bridge", the first and only winning submission for the [Pulitzer Prize for Photography](https://wikipedia.org/wiki/Pulitzer_Prize_for_Photography "Pulitzer Prize for Photography") to have been taken by a woman.

## Year - 1952
